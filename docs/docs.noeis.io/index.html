Noeis Documentation

Think methodology: from information capture to connected insight

Noeis helps researchers, operators, and writers turn scattered reading into usable understanding. The Think methodology combines article capture, concept workspaces, and semantic search so each idea is linked to source evidence and can be revisited later.

What is the Think methodology?

Think is a structured loop for reasoning over real source material, not just generating summaries. It emphasizes traceability, progressive refinement, and reusable knowledge artifacts.

1) Capture

Save articles, highlights, and notes from the web into your library.

2) Connect

Group evidence in concept workspaces to make relationships, gaps, and contradictions visible.

3) Compose

Build outlines and narratives from linked material while preserving provenance.

Concept workspaces: your living map of a topic

A concept workspace is a document-oriented environment where idea blocks, highlights, and references are arranged into a coherent argument or research structure.

  • Outline-first modeling: Organize content into sections that evolve as your thinking matures.
  • Material attachment: Add source highlights and notes directly to the relevant part of the concept.
  • Agent-assisted drafting: Generate suggestions from your library and accept only what is useful.
  • Human-in-the-loop control: AI suggestions remain optional until explicitly accepted.

Semantic search: retrieval by meaning, not exact wording

Traditional search misses relevant evidence when language differs. Noeis semantic search uses embeddings and vector retrieval to find related notes and highlights based on meaning.

  • Cross-phrase matching: Discover sources even when terminology does not exactly match your query.
  • Contextual relevance scores: Prioritize candidates by conceptual closeness.
  • Faster synthesis: Pull related evidence into your workspace before drafting or decision-making.
  • Better recall: Resurface high-value material from your library that would otherwise stay buried.

Why this improves both learning and output quality

The Think + Workspace + Semantic Search stack improves long-term comprehension while reducing repeated work. Instead of re-reading from scratch, you accumulate structured knowledge that compounds over time.
