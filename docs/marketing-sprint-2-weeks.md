# Noeis 2-Week Marketing Sprint

## Constraints
- Founder-led
- Lean execution
- Strong point of view over high-volume generic content
- Jarvis owns X execution once auth exists

## Week 1

### Day 1
- lock canonical positioning language
- align homepage hero with concept-centered thinking and reliable recall
- decide whether external brand should be Noeis-first everywhere now

### Day 2
- prepare 20 X posts across positioning, workflow, AI, reading, and founder POV
- identify 25 reply targets/accounts in Readwise, PKM, research, and AI knowledge-work lanes

### Day 3
- publish one flagship post or thread: capture is solved, recall is not
- publish one product workflow post showing source material -> concept -> reusable insight

### Day 4
- write one essay: most note apps solve capture, not recall
- repurpose into 3 to 5 short X posts

### Day 5
- do one focused engagement block on X: replies, quote-posts, and outreach to aligned accounts
- collect language from responses and refine messaging

### Day 6
- publish one product demo or screenshot walk-through
- publish one founder build-in-public post about why support and contradiction matter

### Day 7
- review best-performing hooks
- update post bank with what resonated

## Week 2

### Day 8
- publish a Readwise-wedge post: importing is not enough, concepts are the next step
- ship one short visual workflow demo

### Day 9
- write one article around concept-first note-taking or AI for reading without losing judgment
- repurpose into social posts

### Day 10
- run another strong reply block on X
- engage with people discussing note-taking, research workflows, and knowledge tools

### Day 11
- publish one sharper POV post attacking weak AI second-brain language
- pair it with a Noeis alternative framing

### Day 12
- publish one founder note on what kind of user Noeis is for and not for

### Day 13
- compile signals: best hooks, best replies, best profile visits, best inbound interest

### Day 14
- decide next repeatable content loops
- identify one or two content themes to double down on

## What Jarvis owns
- X post drafting and queue management
- X reply target selection and engagement strategy
- messaging consistency
- identifying content loops that are working
- turning high-performing ideas into repeatable formats

## What still needs setup
- X auth via xurl
- clear homepage messaging alignment
- optional email capture / waitlist flow
- analytics view for conversion from audience to signup
