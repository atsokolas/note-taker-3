The Architecture of Silence

Investigating the deliberate orchestration of negative space as a structural material in post-secular design.

Silence is rarely the absence of noise; rather, it is a curated state of acoustic and visual stillness. In architectural terms, it is the threshold where the material world ceases its demand for attention. We see this not as a vacuum, but as a deliberate volume.
