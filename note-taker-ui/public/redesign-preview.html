Inside China’s robotics revolution

Chen Liang, the founder of Guchi Robotics, an automation company headquartered in Shanghai, is a calm and understated builder whose enthusiasm comes through most clearly when he is up close with the technology.

For the better part of two decades, he has tried to solve what is, to him, an engineering problem: how to eliminate or liberate as many workers in car factories as technologically possible.

At present, his robots can mount wheels, dashboards, and windows on a car without any human intervention, but the real significance lies in what this reveals about how automation migrates from spectacle into system.
